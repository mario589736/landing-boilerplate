# Conversion Principles

Research-backed principles for optimizing landing page conversion. Use when designing page flow, writing copy, or making UX decisions.

---

## Page flow

The proven high-converting sequence:

```
Nav (sticky, one CTA)
Hero (problem → outcome → action)
Social proof teaser (logos or stat — trust before the scroll)
Features (benefits, not specs)
Testimonials (specific outcomes, real people)
FAQ (remove objections)
Final CTA (mirror the hero)
Footer (legal, nothing else)
```

Do not reorder without a reason and a way to test the reason.

---

## Attention economics

You have 3-7 seconds to communicate value before the visitor leaves.

Priority hierarchy for the fold:
1. What is this? (product category)
2. Who is it for? (audience signal)
3. What do I get? (primary benefit)
4. What do I do next? (one clear CTA)

If the fold cannot answer all four questions clearly, the rest of the page does not matter.

---

## The one-CTA rule

One primary action per viewport. When there are multiple CTAs, users choose none.

Exception: after the fold, a secondary "book a call" CTA alongside a primary "sign up" is acceptable if the audience segments clearly.

---

## Trust signals and their impact

Ranked by effectiveness for B2B SaaS (CXL research):

1. Case studies with specific numbers — highest trust
2. Recognizable customer logos
3. Press mentions ("As seen in...")
4. Testimonials with photo, full name, title
5. Money-back guarantee
6. Security badges (SOC2, GDPR, etc.)
7. Review scores (G2, Capterra, ProductHunt)
8. Follower counts, subscriber numbers — lowest trust

---

## Friction reduction

Every extra step costs conversions. Default to less:

- Fewer form fields → higher completion (3 fields max for top of funnel)
- No forced account creation before value delivery
- Autofocus the first form field
- Show progress on multi-step flows
- Inline validation (not just on submit)
- One-click login (GitHub, Google) where possible

---

## Above-the-fold checklist

Before shipping:

- [ ] Headline names a problem or outcome (no questions, no clever wordplay)
- [ ] Subheadline explains how without jargon
- [ ] One primary CTA with an action verb
- [ ] Visual (product screenshot, illustration, or demo) if relevant
- [ ] Social proof element (logo strip, user count, quote)
- [ ] No navigation links that lead off-page (keep visitors on the page)

---

## Common conversion killers

| Problem | Fix |
|---------|-----|
| Headline is company name | Replace with outcome statement |
| Multiple competing CTAs | Pick one primary action |
| Vague testimonials | Require specific outcomes in quotes |
| Feature list instead of benefit list | Reframe as "so you can…" |
| No pricing page | Add or link pricing — ambiguity kills trust |
| Form with 7+ fields | Cut to 3. Collect the rest after signup. |
| Page loads in 4s+ on mobile | Optimize images, defer scripts |
| No mobile testing | Test on real device before shipping |

---

## A/B testing guidance

Only test one variable at a time. Minimum sample sizes before calling a winner:

- 95% confidence interval
- At least 100 conversions per variant
- At least 2 weeks (to capture weekly patterns)

High-impact elements to test first:
1. Hero headline
2. CTA label
3. CTA placement (above fold vs below first feature section)
4. Social proof position (before or after features)
5. Pricing presentation (monthly vs annual default)

Never test button color before headline — fix the big stuff first.

---

## Analytics setup

Minimum viable measurement:

- Page views + bounce rate by traffic source
- CTA click-through rate
- Form start vs form completion (drop-off)
- Scroll depth (how far do visitors read?)
- Session recordings (Hotjar or equivalent, 100 sessions minimum before drawing conclusions)

Do not build dashboards before having traffic. Get to 500 visits/month first.
